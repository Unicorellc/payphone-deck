03 — The Problem
Miguel mows lawns in Houston. He gets paid in cash — because no one can collect from him digitally. Then he loses $15 every time he sends money home.

05 — Why We Win
We own both sides of the remittance corridor. No competitor can say that.
MOAT 01
Two-Sided Network Effect
A migrant in New York sends money through Payphone. Their mother in Quito receives it in her Payphone wallet and spends it at a Payphone merchant. We capture economics on both ends. Wise, Remitly, and Western Union only process one side. Every new user on either end strengthens the network.
MOAT 02
Tap-to-Phone: The Phone Is the POS Terminal
Every employer's phone becomes a payment terminal. No hardware. No merchant account. A landscaping client taps their card on the employer's phone — the worker gets paid into Payphone instantly. This is the collection infrastructure the informal economy has never had. It turns cash-only jobs into digital-first jobs.
